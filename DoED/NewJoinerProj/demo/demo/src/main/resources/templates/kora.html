The Kora

Click Here To Listen

The kora is a musical instrument from West Africa. It looks like a mix between a harp and a guitar.

The kora has a big round body made from a gourd (a type of fruit) covered with animal skin. It has a long wooden neck and 21 strings.

You play the kora by plucking the strings with your thumbs and fingers. You sit down and hold the kora upright while playing.

The kora makes a beautiful, melodic sound that can be both soft and complex.

The kora is very special in West African culture. People use it to tell stories, sing songs, and celebrate important events.

Some famous kora players are Toumani Diabaté, Ballaké Sissoko, and Sona Jobarteh. They have helped make the kora popular around the world.
